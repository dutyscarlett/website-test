Portfolio

Using digital software, I develop designs that bring people and ideas together. My designs are versatile and flexible, ranging from a yearbook that brought a class together to brand identities and from promotional materials to website designs. Explore my work below.

Design for Justice

The leading question of this project was to be answered with the design and brand identity of a T-shirt and its packaging. The goal was to send a message to a specific audience that promotes one thing I’d like to see change in society. Through this project, I learned how to use Illustrator and some of Photoshop. I also learned how to experiment with text and color and how important feedback is when designing for a large audience.

Wave App

My team was tasked with designing an interface for communication and collaboration among young designers that solved problems present in other collaboration tools. This assignment was great for experimenting with asynchronous collaboration and designing in a team while becoming familiar with the workings of Figma.

WARP Yearbook

A personal project, this yearbook was an attempt to inspire a sense of community and companionship amongst my classmates. The final product was a standard sized 8 page zine. The first four inner pages included photos of the students, staff, and inanimate “characters”. The fifth page included the superlatives, and the sixth page was made up by a collage of the student’s projects. The back cover included the class’ final projects. Once distributed, the students treated the copies like high school yearbooks, passing them around to be signed.

Plantstay Ad Campaign

Tasked with creating a mock ad campaign for a local business, I chose Gainesville’s Plantstay for a campaign aimed at boosting sales. The target audience of this campaign was female college students ages 18-26 living off campus. I took inspiration from Plantstay’s actual advertising to create a sleek and appealing look that would be released in the springtime and again in mid-August, times when people are often looking for plant decor. This project helped me understand various Adobe software and the importance of intentional design.

InsecTech

The primary goal of this project was to build the identity of a fictional organization. This organization, InsecTech, was a science laboratory that experimented with genetic modification on insects. With this concept in mind, I created a logo that incorporates a double-helix and the organization’s name in a way that recalls the 1980’s futuristic sci-fi aesthetic. This concept was used as part of larger project in which a haunted house-style installation was constructed for participants to walk through, experiencing a simulation of walking through a desert filled with mutant insects. This project taught me how to synthesize multiple people’s ideas into one and how to carefully analyze inspiration and references.
